# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

The CyberSkill Design System community is committed to an inclusive, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual orientation.

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, PRs, discussions, RFCs, and any in-person or virtual events organised under the project's name.

## Our standards

**Behaviour we welcome**:

- Using inclusive language.
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Accepting constructive criticism gracefully.
- Focusing on what is best for the community.
- Showing empathy toward other community members.
- Treating disagreement as collaborative — naming the trade-off, not the person.

**Behaviour that is not acceptable**:

- The use of sexualised language or imagery.
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, or personal/political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.
- Using the project to promote dark patterns, deceptive UX, or anti-user practices (these violate the doctrine's `00-audit-and-roadmap.md` §13.2 hard constraint #9).

## Our responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behaviour and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behaviour.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, RFCs, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces and when an individual is officially representing the project (e.g., posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event).

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project team at **zintaen@gmail.com**. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Project maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the project leadership.
